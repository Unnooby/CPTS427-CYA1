# Reflection

## Activity Description
For CYA-1, the expectation was to complete a career-aligned experience totaling approximately 8.64 hours. To meet this requirement, I attended two professional networking events and spent the remaining time developing my resume in Overleaf.

The first event that I attended was a virtual networking event hosted by the WSU Cybersecurity Club, where representatives from Idaho National Laboratories joined via Zoom to connect with students and discuss career opportunities in national security and critical infrastructure research. This event went on for an hour where each member of the zoom call introduced themselves, told us a small amount about their life, and shared how they ended up at INL. Some of the employees included Zaira Rodriguez, Jeff Klinger, Justin Wilson, Eddie Celis, Corri Yanase, and Luis Carrillo. The campus occupies around 890 square-miles of land in southeastern Idaho. There are a total of 17 total locations around the nation, giving me a clearer picture of the scale and scope of national laboratory work.

The second event that I attended was held by SEL in Pullman. I went there to gain insight as to what the daily life look like for a person working at SEL and to understand what can help me stand out during the application process. While I do not have a picture, the room was filled with round tables in the center and outlined in tables with SEL representatives. I listened to speeches for the first hour and networked the rest of the time. The person who stood out to me the most was Nathaniel Jones, who was sitting at a table listed with cybersecurity. This interactiong was, confusing. It did not go as I thought it would. He was primarily focusing on firmware, making a device to ensure that all first responders got the message about an event at the same time. What confused me was that he did not mention many of the applications I thought he would have, such as Splunk. The conversations I had along with the great presentations gave me great insight into what it is like to work at SEL

## Professional Decisions
One key decision I made was choosing to attend networking events rather than immediately applying to jobs or contributing to open source. I felt that at this stage in my career, building connections and understanding what professionals actually do day-to-day was more valuable than sending out cold applications. Hearing directly from INL and SEL employees about their career paths helped me understand that there is no single route into cybersecurity. This both reassured and motivated me.

## Contributions
Both networking events were attended independently. For the resume, all content was researched, written, and formatted by me using Overleaf. I structured it to highlight my education, relevant coursework, technical skills, and projects in a way that reflects my target roles in cybersecurity.

## Quality Assessment
Overall I am satisfied with the progress made, but I recognize there is room for improvement. My resume remains incomplete and will be finalized during CYA-2. If I were to repeat this experience, I would allocate more time upfront to resume development rather than relying on networking events alone to fill the hours. I would also prepare more specific questions before attending networking events to get more targeted value out of each conversation.
